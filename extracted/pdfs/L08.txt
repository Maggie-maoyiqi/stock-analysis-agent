8.1 Introduction
Linear regression models and classification models have been discussed as two separate en-
tities in previous chapters. Situations not yet covered include, e.g.:
‚ÄĘ there exists a mixture of quantitative and qualitative (or categorical) variables in the
dataset;
‚ÄĘ a two-way classification model has unequal sample sizes in treatment groups.
It is desirable to have a universal approach to analysing such complicated data structures.
In general, one can always rewrite (reparameterize) a linear model (linear regression model,
classification model or a mixture of them) to the general form
Y= X ő≤ + őĶ,
by fixing an appropriate design matrix X , and hence, methods developed in Chapters 4 and
5 can be applied directly.

8.4 Representation of two-way classification
If a two-way classification model has a balanced structure, i.e. all treatment groups have the
same number of observations, then it would be most convenient to carry out ANOVA using
techniques given in Section 7.5. (In fact, the techniques given in Section 7.5 cannot be used
for unbalanced structure, even after modifications.) Otherwise, the universal approach given
here is the most appropriate.
