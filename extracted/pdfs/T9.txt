three different years. The energy sources are classified as either traditional (coal, gas) or alterna-
tive (solar, wind). All figures are given in constant 1993 cents per kilowatt-hour. The figures for
the year 2000 are obtained by projection and may be treated as genuine figures.

(a) Using time (of 3 levels: 1985, 1993, 2000) and type of sources (of 2 levels: traditional, al-
ternative) as factors, set up a two-way ANOVA table to test at 5% significance level for any
interaction effects. Explain your findings by means of a visual display.
(b) After testing for interaction effects, test for the two main effects.
(c) Based on the given dataset, Newsweek has drawn the following remark,
"the sun and the wind may be powering our cars and heating our homes sooner than we
think, as the costs of such alternative-energy sources approach those of gas and coal."
Comment.
Hint: Contrast traditional with alternative sources for each year; and then make pairwise
comparisons of such contrasts between 1985 and 1993, and between 1993 and 2000 .
